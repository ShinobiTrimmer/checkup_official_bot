Who Does More At Home?

10 questions. Answer separately — do not show until you've both finished.

Partner 1Partner 2
Main rule: each partner opens this page on their own phone and answers independently.

How to do it

  • 1

    Each partner opens this page on their own phone

  • 2

    Answer all 10 questions independently, no peeking

  • 3

    When you're both done — show your answers at the same time

  • 4

    Do not look for who's right. Just compare.

Part 1 — choose one option
1
How would you estimate your own contribution to household responsibilities?
2
Who in your relationship usually notices first that something needs to be done — before it becomes urgent?
3
When household supplies run out — who notices first?
4
Who more often plans and coordinates shared things — trips, events, celebrations?
5
When your partner takes on a household task — how does it usually happen?
6
Who would feel discomfort first if the house went untended for a few days?
Part 2 — write your answers in your notes app
7
Three things you do regularly that your partner probably doesn't notice.
Write in your notes — and don't show until you're both done.
8
What household responsibility drains you mentally — not physically, but because it's always on you?
Write it in your notes.
9
Have you ever felt like you were managing the home alone, even when your partner was around and helping?
In one sentence, write it in your notes.
10
One thing your partner does quietly and regularly — that you've never said thank you for out loud.
Write it in your notes.

After you've both finished

Add your two numbers from question 1. If the sum is over 100 — that's normal: that's how memory works. Compare questions 7–10 out loud — without judgment, just with curiosity.
