After the Fight: What Happens in the First 5 Minutes?

10 questions. Answer separately — do not show until you've both finished.

Partner 1Partner 2
Main rule: each partner opens this page on their own phone and answers independently.

How to do it

  • 1

    Each partner opens the page on their own phone

  • 2

    Answer all 10 questions independently

  • 3

    When you're both done — show your answers at the same time

  • 4

    Don't look for who's right. Just compare.

Part 1 — choose one option
1
How does a fight usually "end" between you?
2
Who typically makes the first move toward reconciliation?
3
After making up, do you feel like your partner actually understood you?
4
How often do you fight about the same things?
5
What do you usually do right after a fight ends?
6
Do you know what hurts your partner most during a fight?
Part 2 — write your answers in your notes app
7
In one sentence: what do the first five minutes after a fight usually look like between you?
Write honestly in your notes — not how it should be, how it actually is.
8
Is there something you want to say to your partner after a fight — that you never actually say?
Write it in your notes — and don't show until you're both done.
9
What fight has repeated most often in the last year? Name the topic in one word.
Write it in your notes.
10
What would you want your partner to do differently in the first minutes after a conflict?
Write it in your notes.

After you've both finished

Compare questions 1–6 directly. Read questions 7–10 aloud to each other — without judgment, just with curiosity. Pay special attention to question 8: what each of you wanted to say but didn't.
