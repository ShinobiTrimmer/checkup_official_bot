Are We on the Same Page?

10 questions. Answer separately — don't show your answers until you've both finished.

Partner 1 Partner 2

Main rule: each partner opens this page on their own phone and answers independently — don't show your answers to each other until you've both finished all 10 questions.

How to do it

  • 1

    Each partner opens this page on their own phone — or copies the questions into their notes app.

  • 2

    Answer all 10 questions independently, without comparing or peeking at each other's answers.

  • 3

    Agree on a moment when you're both done — then show your answers at the same time.

  • 4

    Don't look for who's right. Just compare — where you matched, where you didn't.

Part 1 — choose one option
1
When did you last feel truly heard by your partner?
2
Who more often notices and handles the small household tasks — shopping, fixing things, paying bills?
3
How does your last argument usually end?
4
How long do you usually need to cool down after a conflict?
5
Do you more often express your feelings directly, or wait for your partner to figure it out?
6
Does your partner notice when you need support — without you having to say it?
Part 2 — write your answers in your notes app
7
What do you think is your partner's greatest contribution to this relationship?
Write one sentence in your notes — and don't show it until your partner has answered too.
8
What do you argue about most often — in one phrase?
Write it in your notes.
9
What does your partner do that you see as care — that they might not even think of as care?
Write it in your notes.
10
If you could change one communication habit between you — what would it be?
Write it in your notes.

After you've both finished

Don't look for who's right. If questions 1–6 matched 4 or more times — you have a solid foundation. Fewer matches isn't a red flag — it's just a conversation that was overdue.

Compare questions 7–10 out loud — without judgment, just with curiosity.
